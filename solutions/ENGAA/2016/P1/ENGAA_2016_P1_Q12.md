## Worked solution

In one complete oscillation the particle travels four amplitudes, so it travels \(4 \times 3.0 = 12\text{ cm}\). In \(60\text{ s}\) at \(5.0\text{ Hz}\), it completes \(300\) oscillations. The total distance is \(300 \times 12 = 3600\text{ cm}\).

## Quickest method

Use distance per cycle \(=4A\), then multiply by the number of cycles.

## Common traps

Using the wavelength instead of the amplitude for the particle's motion.
