## Worked solution

Rearrange the equation:

\[
3x^{2}=(a+2)x-3
\]

to

\[
3x^{2}-(a+2)x+3=0.
\]

For two real distinct roots, the discriminant must be positive:

\[
(a+2)^{2}-4(3)(3)>0.
\]

So

\[
(a+2)^{2}>36
\]

and therefore

\[
|a+2|>6.
\]

This gives

\[
a<-8
\quad\text{or}\quad
a>4.
\]

## Quickest method

Use the discriminant condition \(b^{2}-4ac>0\).

## Common trap

Using \(\ge0\) instead of \(>0\), which would include repeated roots.
