## Worked solution

The uranium nucleus is initially stationary, so the thorium nucleus and alpha particle must have equal and opposite momenta after the decay.

For a fixed momentum,

\[
K=\frac{p^{2}}{2m}
\]

so kinetic energy is inversely proportional to mass.

The alpha particle has mass number \(4\), and the thorium nucleus has mass number \(234\), so the alpha particle receives the fraction

\[
\frac{234}{234+4}=\frac{234}{238}
\]

of the total kinetic energy.

The kinetic energy of the alpha particle is therefore

\[
\frac{234E}{238}
\]

## Quickest method

Use equal momentum after decay, then remember that for equal momentum the lighter particle has the larger kinetic energy.

## Common traps

Dividing the energy in proportion to mass instead of inversely to mass.
