## Worked solution

The radius to a tangent is perpendicular to the tangent. Each triangle formed by \(P\), \(C\), and a tangent point has hypotenuse \(20\) and one side \(10\), so it is a \(30^\circ\)-\(60^\circ\)-\(90^\circ\) triangle.

The two tangent triangles together give the large isosceles triangle area. Subtract the circle sector between the two radii.

This gives

\[
100\sqrt{3}-\frac{100\pi}{3}
=\frac{100}{3}(3\sqrt{3}-\pi).
\]

## Quickest method

Use the right triangle from the centre to a tangent point, then subtract the sector from the triangle.

## Common trap

Subtracting the wrong sector angle, or using only one tangent triangle.
