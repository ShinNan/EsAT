## Worked solution

The first term is \(8\) and the fifth term is \(2\), so

\[
8r^{4}=2.
\]

Hence

\[
r^{4}=\frac{1}{4}
\]

and

\[
r=\pm\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}.
\]

The sixth term is real and positive, so the common ratio must be

\[
r=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}.
\]

The sum to infinity is

\[
\frac{8}{1-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}}=8(2+\sqrt{2}).
\]

## Quickest method

Use the fifth term to find \(r^{4}\), then use the positive sixth term to choose the sign of \(r\).

## Common trap

Selecting the negative ratio, or simplifying the surd fraction incorrectly.
