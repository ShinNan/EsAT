## Worked solution

Let

\[
u=x^{2}.
\]

Then

\[
2x^{4}-9x^{2}+4>0
\]

becomes

\[
2u^{2}-9u+4>0.
\]

Factor:

\[
(2u-1)(u-4)>0.
\]

So

\[
u<\frac{1}{2}
\quad\text{or}\quad
u>4.
\]

Returning to \(x\):

\[
x^{2}<\frac{1}{2}
\quad\text{or}\quad
x^{2}>4.
\]

Therefore

\[
-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}<x<\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}},
\quad x<-2,
\quad\text{or}\quad
x>2.
\]

## Quickest method

Use \(u=x^{2}\), solve the quadratic inequality, then convert back to intervals in \(x\).

## Common trap

Forgetting that \(x^{2}<\frac{1}{2}\) gives a central interval.
