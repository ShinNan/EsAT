## Worked solution

Let the total volume be \(V\).

Tin has volume \(0.1V\), so its mass is \(0.1VY\).

Copper has volume \(0.9V\), so its mass is \(0.9VX\).

The percentage of the mass that is tin is

\[
\frac{0.1VY}{0.1VY+0.9VX}\times100
=\frac{Y}{Y+9X}\times100
\]

This is equivalent to option G.

## Quickest method

Use mass = density \(\times\) volume for each metal, then calculate the tin mass as a percentage of the total mass.

## Common traps

Treating \(10\%\) by volume as \(10\%\) by mass.
