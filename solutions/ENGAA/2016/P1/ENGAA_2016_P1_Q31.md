## Worked solution

The side length of the square is \(1-(-5)=6\), so the circle has radius \(3\).

The square is above the \(x\)-axis, so the circle centre is halfway along \(PQ\) and \(3\) units above the axis:

\[
(-2,3)
\]

The circle equation is

\[
(x+2)^{2}+(y-3)^{2}=9.
\]

Expanding gives

\[
x^{2}+y^{2}+4x-6y+4=0.
\]

## Quickest method

Find the centre and radius, then expand the standard circle equation.

## Common trap

Using the side length as the radius, or placing the circle centre below the \(x\)-axis.
