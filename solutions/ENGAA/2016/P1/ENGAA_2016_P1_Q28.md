## Worked solution

The nearer building is \(48\text{ m}\) away, so its echo travels

\[
2\times48=96\text{ m}
\]

This takes

\[
\frac{96}{320}=0.30\text{ s}
\]

The farther building is \(128-48=80\text{ m}\) away, so its echo travels

\[
2\times80=160\text{ m}
\]

This takes

\[
\frac{160}{320}=0.50\text{ s}
\]

The clicks must have a period that fits both \(0.30\text{ s}\) and \(0.50\text{ s}\). The first such period as the frequency is increased is \(0.10\text{ s}\), so

\[
f=\frac{1}{0.10}=10\text{ Hz}
\]

## Quickest method

Find the two echo times and choose the highest frequency whose period divides both times.

## Common traps

Using one-way distances instead of the out-and-back echo distances.
