## Worked solution

There are \(36\) tennis students. Since \(\frac{2}{3}\) of these are boys, there are \(24\) boys and \(12\) girls who chose tennis.

There are \(46\) girls in total, so the number of girls who chose archery is

\[
46-25-12=9
\]

Since \(27\) students chose archery, \(18\) boys chose archery.

There are \(120-46=74\) boys in total, so the number of boys who chose swimming is

\[
74-24-18=32
\]

The required probability is therefore

\[
\frac{32}{74}=\frac{16}{37}
\]

## Quickest method

Build the two-way table by gender and activity, then divide boys who swim by the total number of boys.

## Common traps

Using \(120\) as the denominator even though the selected student is known to be a boy.
