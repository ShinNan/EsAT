## Worked solution

Use

\[
\tan\theta\sin\theta=\frac{\sin^{2}\theta}{\cos\theta}
=\frac{1-\cos^{2}\theta}{\cos\theta}.
\]

Starting from

\[
7\cos\theta-3\tan\theta\sin\theta=1
\]

and multiplying through by \(\cos\theta\) gives

\[
7\cos^{2}\theta-3(1-\cos^{2}\theta)=\cos\theta.
\]

So

\[
10\cos^{2}\theta-\cos\theta-3=0.
\]

Factor:

\[
(5\cos\theta-3)(2\cos\theta+1)=0.
\]

Therefore

\[
\cos\theta=\frac{3}{5}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\cos\theta=-\frac{1}{2}.
\]

## Quickest method

Convert to a quadratic in \(\cos\theta\), then factor.

## Common trap

Losing the negative sign from \(1-\cos^{2}\theta\).
