## Worked solution

Use (a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2. This gives 3 - 2sqrt(6) + 2 = 5 - 2sqrt(6).

## Quickest method

Square the surds separately and remember the cross term is -2sqrt(3)sqrt(2) = -2sqrt(6).

## Common traps

Dropping the cross term or simplifying sqrt(3)sqrt(2) incorrectly.

## Why the wrong options are wrong

- A. 1 - 2sqrt(6): Uses 3 - 2 = 1 but misses that the square terms add to 5.

- C. 2sqrt(3) - 2sqrt(2): This is twice the original bracket, not its square.

- D. 1: Ignores the cross term.

- E. 5 - 2sqrt(3): The cross term should be -2sqrt(6), not -2sqrt(3).

- F. 13 - 2sqrt(6): Wrong square-term total.

- G. 5 + 2sqrt(6): Cross term has the wrong sign.

- H. 5sqrt(6): Not an expansion of the square.
