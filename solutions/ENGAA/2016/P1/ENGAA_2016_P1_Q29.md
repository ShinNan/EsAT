## Worked solution

Substitute \(x=2\):

\[
8+4p+2q+p^{2}=0
\]

Substitute \(x=1\):

\[
1+p+q+p^{2}=-3.5
\]

From the second equation,

\[
q=-4.5-p-p^{2}
\]

Substitute this into the first equation:

\[
8+4p+2(-4.5-p-p^{2})+p^{2}=0
\]

\[
-1+2p-p^{2}=0
\]

\[
(p-1)^{2}=0
\]

So

\[
p=1
\]

## Quickest method

Use the two substitutions to form two equations, then eliminate \(q\).

## Common traps

Forgetting the \(p^{2}\) term when substituting either value of \(x\).
