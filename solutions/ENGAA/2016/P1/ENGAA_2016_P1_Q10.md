## Worked solution

The radius factor contributes 100^{2} = 10 000. The temperature factor contributes (1/2)^{4} = 1/16. The net factor is 10 000/16 = 625. Therefore the new power is 4.0 \times 10^{26} x 625 = 2.5 \times 10^{29} W.

## Quickest method

Compute the proportional change only: 100^{2} divided by 2^{4} equals 625, then multiply 4.0 \times 10^{26} by 625.

## Common traps

Forgetting that temperature is raised to the fourth power, or applying the radius factor only once instead of squaring it.

## Why the wrong options are wrong

- A. 2.5 x 10^27 W: Uses too small a scale factor.

- B. 1.0 x 10^28 W: Uses an incorrect power of ten after scaling.

- C. 2.0 x 10^28 W: Close power-of-ten distractor but half the correct value.

- E. 1.0 x 10^30 W: Too large by a factor of 4.

- F. 2.0 x 10^30 W: Too large by a factor of 8.

- G. 2.5 x 10^33 W: Ignores or mishandles the temperature decrease.

- H. 1.0 x 10^34 W: Applies the radius increase without the T^4 decrease.
