## Worked solution

Newton's Third Law pairs are equal and opposite forces acting on different bodies.

The force from the parachutist on the parachute and the force from the parachute on the parachutist form an action-reaction pair.

These are represented by \(N\) and \(P\), so

\[
N=P
\]

This is equation 4 only.

Equations 1 and 2 are force-balance equations for terminal speed. They are not direct Newton's Third Law pairs.

## Quickest method

Look for equal and opposite forces between the parachute and the parachutist. That identifies equation 4.

## Common traps

Choosing equations that describe terminal-speed equilibrium rather than Third Law interaction pairs.
