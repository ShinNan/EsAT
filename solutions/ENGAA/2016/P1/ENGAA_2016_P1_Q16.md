## Worked solution

The current is \(I=\frac{V}{R}=\frac{6.0}{15}=0.40\text{ A}\). Over \(3.0\) minutes, \(t=180\text{ s}\), so \(Q=It=0.40\times180=72\text{ C}\). For the heater, \(V=\frac{W}{Q}=\frac{180}{72}=2.5\text{ V}\).

## Quickest method

Use the total resistance to find current, then use the heater energy to find the heater voltage.

## Common traps

Using the full \(6.0\text{ V}\) battery voltage as the heater voltage.
