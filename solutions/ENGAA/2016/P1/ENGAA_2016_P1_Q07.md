## Worked solution

The original total age is 20 x 28 = 560. With 22 members the new mean is 30, so the new total is 22 x 30 = 660. The two new members therefore total 100 years, giving a mean of 50 years.

## Quickest method

Compare total ages before and after: 660 - 560 = 100 for two people.

## Common traps

Adding 2 years to 28 and assuming the new members average 30, which ignores the existing 20 members.

## Why the wrong options are wrong

- A. 20 years: Too low; it would reduce the mean.

- B. 22 years: Too low; it would reduce the mean.

- C. 30 years: This is the new overall mean, not the mean of the two new members.

- D. 40 years: This does not add enough total age to raise the existing group mean by 2.

- F. 52 years: Slightly too high; it would give a total new age of 104.
