## Worked solution

Translating \(y=x^{2}\) by

\[
\begin{pmatrix}4\\3\end{pmatrix}
\]

gives

\[
y=(x-4)^{2}+3.
\]

Reflection in the horizontal line \(y=-1\) maps a \(y\)-value to

\[
-2-y.
\]

So the reflected curve is

\[
y=-2-\left((x-4)^{2}+3\right)
\]

which simplifies to

\[
y=-5-(x-4)^{2}.
\]

## Quickest method

Track the vertex: \((0,0)\to(4,3)\), then reflect \((4,3)\) across \(y=-1\) to \((4,-5)\).

## Common trap

Reflecting in the \(x\)-axis instead of in the line \(y=-1\).
