## Worked solution

After one year the value is 80% of £15 000, and after two years it is 0.8^2 x 15000 = £9600. The reduction is £15 000 - £9600 = £5400.

## Quickest method

Use the multiplier 0.8 twice, then subtract the remaining value from the original price.

## Common traps

Subtracting 40% of the original price; successive 20% decreases compound, so the second decrease is 20% of the reduced value.

## Why the wrong options are wrong

- A. £600: Too small by a factor of 9.

- B. £3000: This is only the first year's depreciation.

- D. £6000: This treats the total depreciation as 40% of the original value.

- E. £9000: This is the wrong remaining/reduced value relationship.

- F. £9600: This is the value after two years, not the reduction.

- G. £12 000: This is 80% of the original value, not the two-year reduction.
