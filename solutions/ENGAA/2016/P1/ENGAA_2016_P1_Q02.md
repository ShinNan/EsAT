## Worked solution

The mass number falls by 4 while the atomic number is unchanged. One alpha emission changes A by -4 and Z by -2; two beta-minus emissions then increase Z by 2, restoring the atomic number to 82.

## Quickest method

Match mass first: a drop of 4 means one alpha. Then restore proton number with two beta-minus decays.

## Common traps

Counting only the mass change and forgetting that alpha decay also changes the atomic number.

## Why the wrong options are wrong

- A. 3 alpha: Three alpha emissions reduce mass by 12 and atomic number by 6.

- B. 2 alpha and 1 beta: Two alpha emissions reduce mass by 8.

- C. 2 alpha and 2 beta: Two alpha emissions reduce mass by 8.

- E. 3 beta: Beta emissions do not reduce mass number by 4.
