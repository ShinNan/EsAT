## Worked solution

For a regular polygon with \(n\) sides, the exterior angle is

\[
\frac{360}{n}
\]

Since \(PQ\) is extended to \(T\), the angle at \(Q\) in triangle \(QRT\) is this exterior angle.

The triangle \(QRT\) is isosceles because \(RQ=RT\), so the two base angles are equal. Therefore

\[
x + 2\left(\frac{360}{n}\right)=180
\]

Rearranging gives

\[
n=\frac{720}{180-x}
\]

## Quickest method

Use the exterior angle of the regular polygon and the isosceles triangle angle sum.

## Common traps

Using the polygon interior angle instead of the exterior angle at \(Q\).
